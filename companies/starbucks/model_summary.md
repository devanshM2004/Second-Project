# Starbucks Model Summary

## Project Objective

The purpose of this case study is to build a simplified 3-statement financial model for Starbucks in order to better understand how business activity flows through the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement.

## Key Assumptions

* Revenue = $100.00
* Cost of Goods Sold = 35.00% of revenue
* Operating Expenses = 30.00% of revenue
* Depreciation = 5.00% of revenue
* Interest Expense = $3.00
* Tax Rate = 25.00%

## Statement Highlights

### Income Statement

The model generates:

* Revenue of $100.00
* Operating Income of $30.00
* Net Income of $20.25

### Balance Sheet

The model shows:

* Total Assets of $68.00
* Total Liabilities of $26.00
* Equity of $42.00

The balance sheet balances because:

* Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Equity

### Cash Flow Statement

The model shows:

* Cash Flow from Operations = $18.25
* Cash Flow from Investing = ($6.00)
* Cash Flow from Financing = $2.75
* Net Change in Cash = $15.00
* Ending Cash = $15.00

## How the Statements Connect

* Revenue and expenses drive Net Income on the income statement
* Net Income flows into the cash flow statement
* The cash flow statement adjusts for non-cash items and working capital changes
* Ending Cash from the cash flow statement appears on the balance sheet

## What This Model Demonstrates

This model demonstrates a practical understanding of:

* financial statement structure
* profitability analysis
* balance sheet mechanics
* cash flow logic
* the use of AI to accelerate finance learning and project development

## Limitations

This is a simplified educational model. It is designed to demonstrate structure and logic, not to replicate Starbucks' exact reported financial statements.
