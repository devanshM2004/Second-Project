# Starbucks Financial Model Assumptions

## Overview
This document outlines the key assumptions used to build a simplified financial model for Starbucks. These assumptions are used to construct the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement.

All figures are estimates for learning purposes and are not intended to reflect exact financial data.

## Revenue Assumptions
- Revenue growth rate: 8.00% annually
- Primary driver: same-store sales growth + new store openings
- Revenue streams:
  - Company-operated stores
  - Licensed stores
  - Consumer packaged goods

## Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
- COGS estimated at 35.00% of revenue
- Includes:
  - Coffee beans and raw materials
  - Packaging
  - Direct labor

## Operating Expenses
- SG&A estimated at 30.00% of revenue
- Includes:
  - Rent
  - Marketing
  - Corporate overhead

## Depreciation & Capital Expenditures
- Depreciation: 5.00% of revenue
- CapEx: 6.00% of revenue

## Taxes
- Effective tax rate: 25.00%

## Working Capital Assumptions
- Accounts Receivable: 5.00% of revenue
- Inventory: 8.00% of revenue
- Accounts Payable: 6.00% of revenue

## Debt & Financing
- Assume moderate leverage
- Interest expense estimated at 2.00% of total debt

## Notes
- All assumptions are simplified for modeling purposes
- Values will be refined as the model becomes more detailed
