# Bank of America – Loan Case Study (Simplified)

## Scenario

A borrower applies for a loan with the following profile:

* Annual income: $80.00
* Monthly expenses: $3.00
* Existing debt payments: $1.00
* Cash savings: $10.00
* Loan requested: $20.00

## Step 1: Cash Flow Analysis

Monthly income:
$80.00 / 12 = $6.67

Total monthly obligations:
Expenses ($3.00) + Debt ($1.00) = $4.00

Remaining cash flow:
$6.67 - $4.00 = $2.67

## Step 2: Debt Burden

Debt-to-income perspective:
Existing debt + new loan increases fixed obligations.

Key idea:
Higher fixed payments reduce flexibility and increase default risk.

## Step 3: Liquidity

Borrower has $10.00 in savings.

This provides a buffer in case of income disruption.

## Step 4: Risk Assessment

Strengths:

* Positive monthly cash flow
* Some liquidity buffer

Weaknesses:

* Moderate existing debt
* Limited margin for unexpected expenses

## Step 5: Lending Decision (Simplified)

Decision: APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS

Possible conditions:

* Higher interest rate
* Lower loan amount
* Additional documentation

## Key Takeaways

* Credit decisions are based on cash flow, not just income
* Liquidity provides downside protection
* Risk is managed through pricing and structure, not just approval/denial

## Why This Matters

This exercise demonstrates how lenders evaluate borrower risk using basic financial information and structured decision-making.

It reflects the type of thinking used in credit risk and underwriting roles.
