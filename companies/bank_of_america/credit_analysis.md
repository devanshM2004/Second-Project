# Bank of America Credit Risk Analysis (Simplified)

## Objective

The goal of this project is to understand how banks like Bank of America evaluate credit risk and make lending decisions.

This is a simplified, educational case study designed to explore how financial data and assumptions are used to assess borrower risk.

## Key Credit Risk Factors

### 1. Ability to Pay

The most important factor in credit analysis is whether the borrower can generate enough income to repay debt.

This is typically evaluated using:

* income vs expenses
* cash flow stability
* debt service coverage

### 2. Leverage

Leverage measures how much debt a borrower has relative to their income or assets.

Higher leverage = higher risk

Key idea:

* too much debt reduces flexibility and increases default risk

### 3. Liquidity

Liquidity refers to how easily a borrower can access cash to meet short-term obligations.

Examples:

* cash balances
* liquid assets

Low liquidity = higher risk

### 4. Credit History

Past behavior is a strong indicator of future behavior.

Lenders evaluate:

* repayment history
* defaults or delinquencies
* consistency over time

### 5. Economic Conditions

External factors also affect risk:

* interest rates
* unemployment
* economic cycles

Even strong borrowers can become risky in weak economic environments.

## Simplified Lending Decision Framework

A lender like Bank of America would typically:

1. analyze borrower income and cash flow
2. evaluate total debt obligations
3. assess collateral (if applicable)
4. determine risk level
5. price the loan based on risk
6. approve or reject the application

## Key Takeaways

* Credit risk is about probability of repayment, not just income level
* Cash flow is more important than accounting profit
* Risk is influenced by both borrower-specific and macroeconomic factors
* Banks price risk through interest rates and loan terms
